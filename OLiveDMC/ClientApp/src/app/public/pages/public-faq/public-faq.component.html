PPC can work in distinct ways, based upon your advertisement network and advertising type. By way of example, while similar, Microsoft Advertising and Google Ads use different algorithms for their advertising auctions. When discussing how PPC advertising functions, most refer to Google's procedure

So, if you advertise on Google, you can anticipate the following:
  • A user searches on an internet search engine such as Google
  • Google launches the ad auction, compiling all pertinent ads and their Quality Scores
  • The voucher appears at every ad's bid, quality, and potential impact to assign an Advertisement Rank
  • Google delivers the advertisements in order of highest Ad Rank to lowest Ad Rank

With the following formula, Google also computes your CPC throughout the ad auction:

CPC = Advertisement Rank of this advertisement below you Your Quality score + $0.01

This formula is the reason why PPC consultants, as well as agencies, focus on generating high quality. If you desire to construct an engaging advertisement, you can attain a more top quality score, which can result in lower costs and an improved Ad Rank.

Why is the click-through rate significantly less than Google's of Facebook?

The average CTR of a Facebook advertisement is approximately 0.05%. The average CTR of a Google display ad is around 0.4% -- nearly ten times higher. Search ads have CTR. There might be several reasons Facebook's advertising CTR might be comparatively low:

  • Facebook's advertising formats are more restrictive and less engaging than Google's.
  • Facebook ads are more clearly ads, whereas Google ads blend in more seamlessly with all the organic results.
  • Folks aren't in shopping mode when using Facebook.
